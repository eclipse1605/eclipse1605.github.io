## 16th May - A first working PrO posterior experiment

I worked on a simple PoC implementation of the PrO posterior update on synthetic data. To keep things easy to inspect, I generated a 3D Gaussian mixture and used it as the observed dataset. Starting from randomly initialized particles, I then ran the particle update with the log-score WGF interaction term, combined with the prior gradient and a small Gaussian noise term.

The main goal of this notebook was to check whether the update was moving particles in a sensible direction. The 1D histograms showed that the particle marginals quickly moved away from the initial random state and began matching the structure of the data. In the pairwise scatter plots, the final particles concentrated around the same three clusters visible in the observations, which was a reassuring sign that the posterior approximation was capturing the multimodal structure of the target distribution.

The trajectory plots also showed an initial adjustment phase, followed by much more stable particle motion once the particles had found the main regions of mass. The diagnostic plots confirmed this behavior. The spread of the particle system increased early on and then settled, while the norms of the WGF term and the overall drift dropped from their initial large values to a more stable regime. Thankfully, I did not see any non-finite log-density values, which means the numerical implementation was behaving well. One thing that stood out was that the ratio clipping became increasingly active over time, so that will be important to keep an eye on as I refine this.

It seems as though this was a pretty useful first sanity check. The notebook suggests that my understanding is heading in the right direction, and that the PrO posterior is at least able to recover the coarse multimodal structure of the synthetic target.
